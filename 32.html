This means that we start with low- level details like comments, identifiers, variables, and types; then build to expressions, statements, objects, and functions; and then cover high-level language abstractions like classes and modules. I take the word deﬁnitive in the title of this book seriously, and the coming chapters explain the language at a level of detail that may feel off- putting at first. True mastery of JavaScript requires an understanding of the details, however, and I hope that you will make time to read this book cover to cover. But please don’t feel that you need to do that on your first reading. If you find yourself feeling bogged down in a section, simply skip to the next.
